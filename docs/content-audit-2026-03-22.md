# Content Audit & Suggested Changes

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Audited against:** Alex's selling framework (outcomes > products, relationships, authenticity, team development)

---

## 1. Sell outcomes on the engagement models

**Where:** Home page "How this works" section + Work With Me engagement cards

**Problem:** The copy describes what Alex does (embeds 1-2 days/week, attends meetings, helps with hiring) but not what the founder gets. A prospect reading this knows you understand their pain but can't clearly picture the after state.

**Suggested changes:**

### Home page — "How this works" steps

Add a one-line outcome under each step description:

- **Embedded support:** After the current description, add something like: *"Most founders tell me the biggest shift isn't technical — it's the confidence to stop second-guessing every engineering decision."*
- **Transformation project:** After the current description, add: *"You finish with a team that ships faster, a hire you trust, or both."*

### Work With Me — engagement cards

Add an "Outcome" line to each card, below the description:

- **Fractional CTO:** *"You get a technical co-pilot who makes the calls you can't make alone — and builds the team so you won't need me."*
- **Technical Advisory:** *"You finish with a clear technical roadmap, the right hire in place, or systems that run without a CTO."*

---

## 2. Surface insights on the homepage

**Where:** Home page, between the testimonial and the final CTA

**Problem:** The insights section is disconnected from the sales flow. Prospects who land on the homepage may never click through to insights, missing content that builds trust and demonstrates active thinking. Your selling framework says: *"Understand your prospects deeply. Make them like you."* The insights do this — but only if people see them.

**Suggested change:**

Add a "Latest thinking" section with 2-3 recent insight cards (title + one-line excerpt + link). Keep it lightweight — not a full feed, just a teaser that says "this person is actively thinking about the problems I face."

Example:

```
Latest thinking
───────────────
[Empathy Is The Hard Problem]     [Strategy Should Compound]     [Restraint Is a Superpower]
Brief excerpt...                   Brief excerpt...               Brief excerpt...
```

---

## 3. Make "engineering himself out of the job" a visible thread

**Where:** Home page credibility section or "How this works"

**Problem:** The headline — "engineering himself out of the job" — is the single most differentiating line on the site. It implies team development and capability building, which maps directly to your framework's "Train others to replicate your success." But nothing else on the site reinforces this idea. It's a promise in the headline that the body copy doesn't cash.

**Suggested changes (pick one or both):**

### Option A: Add a line to the credibility section

After "Now I help Series A founders build engineering teams that scale," add: *"My job is to make the decisions you can't make yet — then make sure your team can make them without me."*

### Option B: Add it to the "What to expect" list on Work With Me

Add a fifth bullet: *"Capability transfer — I don't just make decisions, I teach your team to make them. The goal is always independence."*

---

## 4. Add more testimonials

**Where:** Home page and/or Work With Me page

**Problem:** Two testimonials is the minimum for credibility. Three to four, from different contexts, would meaningfully strengthen social proof and show range.

**Ideal mix:**

| Context | What it proves |
|---------|---------------|
| Non-technical founder (Dennis - already have) | "I can trust this person with tech I don't understand" |
| CEO with existing team (Andrew - already have) | "He integrates, doesn't disrupt" |
| **CTO or engineering lead Alex mentored** | Proves the "out of the job" promise — someone who grew because of Alex |
| **Investor or board member** | "The technical leadership gave us confidence to invest/continue" |

**Action:** Source 1-2 more testimonials. A mentored CTO quote would be the strongest addition because it directly proves the unique selling proposition.

---

## 5. Consider an About page (lower priority)

**Where:** New page at `/about`

**Problem:** There's no dedicated space for Alex's story. The credibility section on the homepage does some of this (Ecologi, Just, LinkedIn ranking), but it's compressed into a sales context. An about page lets a prospect who's already interested go deeper — the snowboarding story from "The Rebuild Is The Strategy," the hyperbaric chamber from "Healthy Teams Outperform" — these are relationship-building details that don't fit in the sales flow but make a prospect think "I'd enjoy working with this person."

**What it would contain:**
- The professional arc (co-founder/CTO at Ecologi, CTO at Just, now fractional)
- What drives the work (not a mission statement — a genuine paragraph about why this and why now)
- The personal edge (Sheffield, physical health focus, direct communication style)
- Photo

**Why lower priority:** The current site converts without it. The insights already reveal personality. But if conversion ever feels flat, this is the first thing to add.
